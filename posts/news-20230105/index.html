    China reversed its zero covid policy #

    By the end of an otherwise triumphant Communist Party Congress for Xi Jinping in October, it became harder for the government to prolong its zero covid policy.

    The containment meansure has come at a high cost, cutting sharply into the nation’s exports and retail sales, draining local finances and driving parts of the population to a near-breaking point.

    As late as mid-November, Xi was still wavering on whether and how to unwind a policy he had so closely associated himeself.

    Shortly after, a rare wave of protests burst out across the major cities in China. These protests, along with urgent pleas from many corners of the government, finally prodded Xi to shift his policy.

    As the policy was suddenly scrapped in early December, it thrust China into a new emergency.

    The abrupt reversal of policy left the whole country unprepared, facing a spike in patients with no stockpiles of medical essentials.
